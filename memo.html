// section.01 — the vision

What we're actually building.

Sm0kBot is the smart cigar & tobacco vending platform for hotels, lounges, casinos, and members-only venues. Biometric age verification on every transaction. Zero capital cost to the venue. We place, stock, and operate.

The long game: a multi-state network of Sm0kBot machines and a brand recognizable enough to be acquired by a major tobacco company or scaled into a franchise model. We're not building a small business. We're building the category — and we want both founders aligned for that timeline.

// section.02 — the deal

The structure: 50/50 founding partners.

  • Equity split: 50% / 50% membership interests. Equal partners, equal upside, equal voice.
  • Capital contribution at formation: Sweat equity from both founders. No cash buy-in required from either side.
  • Reserve pool: Up to 10% set aside for future hires, advisors, or specialists. Both founders dilute proportionally when granted.
  • Matching-money rule: If one founder injects cash, the other matches dollar-for-dollar within 90 days, OR equity adjusts to reflect the difference. Nobody buys control via capital.

// section.04 — decision making

How we move without locking up.

Defined lanes. Each founder has final authority on decisions within their lane. The other consults; doesn't veto.

  • Mike's lane (CEO): brand, marketing, product mix, customer relationships, public face, lifestyle vertical, tobacco/cigar partnerships
  • Christian's lane (COO/GC): legal, regulatory, lobbying, LLC operations, contracts, gov affairs, banking operations (account management, vendor payments under threshold, tax filings, bookkeeper supervision)

Joint authority (both sign): equity issuance, capital raises over $25K, company sale, C-level hires, annual budget, loans over $25K, opening a new state market, spending or transfers from company accounts above $5,000 per transaction or $15,000 per month.

Financial transparency baseline: dual-signatory on all company bank accounts at the threshold above. Both founders have full read-only access to every account, statement, and ledger at all times. Monthly financial reports shared between founders within 5 business days of month-end. Standard founder protection — no surprises, no unilateral fund movements above threshold.

Audit rights. Either founder may, at any time, engage an independent third-party CPA or auditor to review the LLC's books, records, accounts, and financial transactions. The cost is borne by the LLC if no irregularity is found; by the responsible founder if irregularity is confirmed. An annual independent financial review is baked into the standard annual budget regardless. This isn't about distrust — it's the financial hygiene every multi-founder business needs to grow, raise capital, and survive due diligence at acquisition.

If we get stuck on a joint decision: 7-day cool-off → independent advisor mediation (pre-named: a trusted mutual contact) → if still locked, either founder can invoke a "shotgun" — name a price; the other either buys at that price or sells at that price. Forces fair valuation. Nobody can hold the company hostage.

// section.05 — protecting the work

Vesting + time commitment.

This protects both of us. If either of us has to step back for a life event, the unvested equity returns to the company — no hard feelings, just structure.

  • 4-year vesting applied to both founders' equity
  • 12-month cliff — nothing vests if a founder leaves in year one
  • After cliff: 25% vested, then 1/48 per month over 36 months
  • Sale accelerator: 100% vests immediately on company sale or acquisition
  • Minimum time commitment: Each founder commits a defined minimum of hours/week to Sm0kBot. If either drops below for 90+ days without notice, equity reverts proportionally.
// section.06 — the exit

Built for sale. Designed clean.

We're transparent about the long-game intent: we want this company sellable to a major tobacco distributor (Philip Morris, Altria, etc.) or expandable into a franchise model. That changes how we structure from day one.

  • Drag-along: If a qualifying offer comes in above an agreed valuation threshold, either founder can force the sale. Neither of us can block a life-changing exit out of emotion.
  • Tag-along: If one founder gets a buyout offer for their stake, the other has the right to sell their stake at the same per-share price. Prevents an acquirer from peeling one of us off and stranding the other.
  • IP & relationships assignment: All work product, contacts, deals, and brand IP generated during the partnership belong to Sm0kBot LLC. Pre-existing relationships catalogued at formation; post-formation: LLC property.
  • Liquidation waterfall: At sale, each founder first recoups any matched capital they contributed. Remaining proceeds split 50/50.
// section.07 — the rest

The clauses we'll lock in the formal agreement.

These are the items that need real legal language but the principle is straightforward:

  • Buy-Sell + Death/Disability: If one of us dies or becomes disabled, the other has right of first refusal on the stake at a pre-agreed valuation. LLC-funded life insurance on both of us to provide buyout liquidity.
  • Non-compete during partnership: Neither runs a competing tobacco-vending business while in Sm0kBot. Christian keeps Innovative Strategies LLC running (different vertical, no conflict).
  • Confidentiality: Trade secrets, vendor lists, customer contracts protected during + post-partnership.
  • Regulatory compliance: FDA Tobacco Retailer registration, Tobacco 21 federal law, state licensing maintenance, biometric data handling under CCPA (Identivend stack already compliant).
  • Dispute resolution: Arbitration (JAMS or AAA) before litigation. Defined venue.
  • State of formation: California by default; open to Delaware if there's a tax/M&A reason — your call, Christian, since you know the filing landscape best.
